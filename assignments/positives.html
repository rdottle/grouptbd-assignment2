Positives

What’s great about this project is that you can see how two people took the same theme and created totally different visualizations. Both data visualizations are thematic (i.e. use of lotr fonts; flowers to symbolize summer). They showcase the creators’ own aesthetic styles. Formatted like a diary entry, complete with sketches and links to the datasets, over the course of the project to give a sense of the process. They show us sketches of different avenues that they explored. This narrative diary format gives them the opportunity to give the rationale for the decisions they make.

Who’s speaking in Middle Earth?:

"There was definitely a need to create empty space above and below the inner section. Not only to have a less squished feeling, but also to give the strings in that region the room to really flow in a nice S-shape.”

Film flowers:

“Though in the beginning I was much more excited about the fireworks, I ultimately decided that the flowers were much prettier and more fun to code. It was also more straightforward; I wasn't as sure about the potential math the fireworks would require (I thought maybe I could do something with the gravity function of the force layout over time)”
